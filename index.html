Since time immemorial, humans have looked to others to gauge their standing. In today's interconnected world, this deep tendency, felt acutely on campus, takes on new significance.

This 'Comparison Virus' is the pervasive urge to evaluate ourselves against peers in grades, achievements, internships, and social recognition.

Is this constant assessment a silent motivator, or a source of profound misery? Our study investigates this dual nature, understanding how comparisons shape mental well-being on campus, amplified by social media.

IIT Kharagpur context: Students reported frequent comparison hotspots — CGPA & Double Major discussions, society selections and GC outcomes, 3rd‑year internship races, high‑visibility LinkedIn wins, and the placement‑season scoreboard. The insights below summarise responses from our campus questionnaire.

The Drive to Thrive: Comparison as a Motivator

Comparison can be a powerful force for self-improvement and goal attainment.

  • Goal Setting & Ambition: Peer achievements ignite ambition, setting clear benchmarks.
    "Friend's Dream-11 style internship pushed me to polish my resume and apply."
  • Performance Enhancement: Observing success inspires better habits and strategies.
    "A classmate’s 9+ CGPA study routine helped me tighten my prep."
  • Social Learning: Comparison guides efforts by understanding what's 'achievable' in KGP’s context.
    "Seniors’ POR journeys helped me plan which societies and GCs to target."
  • Validation & Belonging: Favorable comparison reinforces competence and positive self-perception.
    "Wing outcomes similar to mine made me feel I truly belong here."
  • Inspiring Role Models: Successful peers serve as relatable role models.
    "A senior’s route to top grad school made it feel possible for me too."
  • Discovering Interests: Seeing peers try new things can expose you to new passions.
    "I joined the coding club after seeing my roommate's project; I love it now."

The Trap of Despair: Comparison as Misery

Unmanaged comparison leads to psychological distress and hinders well-being.

  • Anxiety & Stress: Constant measurement fuels worry about not being 'good enough.'
    "LinkedIn during placements makes me panic; everyone seems ahead."
  • Lowered Self-Esteem: Perceived deficiencies erode self-confidence and foster inadequacy.
    "Friends with near-perfect CGPAs make my effort feel invisible."
  • Envy & Isolation: Comparison breeds negative emotions, damaging connections.
    "It's tough to celebrate others when I'm secretly comparing outcomes."
  • Burnout & Exhaustion: Pressure to 'keep up' leads to unsustainable pace.
    "I'm drained trying to match everyone’s societies and projects."
  • Mental Health Impact: Contributes to imposter syndrome and depression.
    "Despite accomplishments, I feel I don't deserve to be here, comparing to 'smarter' people."
  • Decision Paralysis: Fear of making the 'wrong' choice compared to peers.
    "Stuck choosing electives because I worry what looks the ‘best.’"

The Amplifiers: How Context Shapes Comparison

External factors intensify or mediate comparison's effects.

  • Social Media: Curated realities create skewed comparisons, intensifying inadequacy.
    "Everyone's travel or internship posts make my semester feel mediocre."
  • Peer Culture: Competitive campus culture amplifies both motivational and detrimental comparisons.
    "Pressure to discuss grades after exams makes it hard to focus on my own learning."
  • Personal Disposition: Individual traits and coping mechanisms shape reactions to comparison.
    "Some thrive on competition, others find it crippling; internal dialogue is key."
  • Institutional Pressure: Systems like grading curves or rankings force direct comparison.
    "Being graded on a curve means I'm not just learning; I'm actively competing with friends."

Turning Awareness into Action: Coping with Comparison

Practical strategies you can try during internships, placements, or exam seasons.

  • Mindful Noticing: Catch comparison as it occurs; name it without judgment.
  • Progress over Optics: Track your own weekly progress rather than others’ outcomes.
  • Digital Hygiene: Mute triggering feeds during crunch periods; set usage windows.
  • Peer Support: Normalize honest check-ins in hostels and societies; create “authenticity hours.”
  • Role Model Audit: Follow seniors who share process, not just outcomes.
  • Help-Seeking: Reach out to the Counselling Centre, faculty advisors, or trusted peers early.

Reflecting on the "Virus"

The 'Comparison Virus' is intrinsic to human experience, especially on campus. Recognizing its dual potential—to inspire or to inhibit—is the first step towards conscious navigation and genuine well-being.

Maybe the race was never against others, but against yesterday’s version of ourselves.
